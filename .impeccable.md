## Design Context

### Users

Core users are employees of freight forwarding companies, working in daily operational contexts such as order entry, booking coordination, customs-related workflows, and fee management. Their primary job-to-be-done is to complete high-frequency, detail-heavy business tasks accurately and efficiently while keeping process visibility clear.

### Brand Personality

The product should feel professional, reliable, efficient, and visually polished. The emotional target is confidence and clarity: users should feel the system is stable, trustworthy, and easy to process during long work sessions.

### Aesthetic Direction

Preferred direction is Apple-inspired minimalism: clean, modern, and elegant with restrained visual language. The interface should avoid flashy or decorative-heavy styling and stay suitable for management-system usage. Theme strategy is light mode only. No explicit benchmark products are provided, and no hard accessibility compliance target is currently specified.

### Design Principles

1. Clarity over decoration: prioritize readable information hierarchy and task comprehension over ornamental visuals.
2. Efficiency first: reduce cognitive load and interaction friction for repetitive operational workflows.
3. Professional consistency: maintain predictable spacing, typography, and component behavior across modules.
4. Calm visual tone: keep the UI concise and aesthetically pleasing without "busy" or overly animated presentation.
5. Stable trust signals: emphasize reliability through clear states, deterministic feedback, and low visual noise.
